About Us

R V Coding Club was started in the year 2016 by six students from the ECE department with an aim of bridging the gap between the departments when it comes to competitive coding. Then known as InSYNC, the R V Coding Club bloomed from a small team of 10 individuals to a capacity of over 60 students today, spanning across the branches of CSE, ISE, ECE, EEE and Mechanical among others

Code Marathon

May / August 2019

On 18th August 2019, the Coding Club held its 2nd edition of the Coding Marathon. It was an 8 hour long workshop where the students were made to work on brainstorming problems from the field of Data Structues and Algorithms. The sessions covered all topics ranging from basics to complex algorithms and data structures. This event was aimed at motivating students to take part in various competitions like ACM ICPC etc. The sessions were conducted by the final year students of the club who have represented the college at various competitions. .

RVCE HACKS

september 2019

RVCE Hacks! was an intra-collegiate coding competition open to students of all branches and semesters. It is the Coding Club’s first ever event. The preliminary round was held at the IEM Auditorium on 17th January, 2017. The top 40 participants qualified for a second coding round, held on 21st January, 2017 at the ISE department. The event saw wide participation from students across semesters and branches, with 236 participants. Participants were from ISE, CSE, Mechanical, Civil and EC departments. The preliminary round was a logical one, with many puzzles and brain teasers. The coding round was a competition hosted on Hackerrank with participants showcasing their programming prowess trying to make it to the top of the leaderboard. The event was aimed at creating and fostering interest in puzzle solving and coding on campus..

Workshops

Coding Club of RVCE inspires students of all departments to learn coding, compete in events and competitions.In line with this thinking, it was decided to have a weekly sessions every Wednesday on a certain topic of competitive coding to expand our knowledge in this area. The sessions held so far have been on – Time and space complexity , Numerical algorithms,Hashing , Dynamic programming ,Competitive coding and problem solving strategies ,Problem solving session I and II ,Cyber security.

Talk By Wolfgang Richter

He is the Head of Infrastructure and Security at Sorocco Pvt Ltd. He is has a undergraduate degree in Computer Science from University of Virginia and has a Masters in Computer Science from Carniege Mellon University, Cambridge. Having worked with technology giant like IBM , he talked about his PhD Thesis to us and also explained the importance of different fields of Computer Science such as Distributed Systems , AI and Software Administration..

Talk By Anup Kalbalia

Anup Kalbalia is the head of Codechef. He was invited to speak at our college and address the students on the importance of problem solving ability. The title of his talk was ‘Stories from the world of Competitive Programming’. He spoke about India’s position in the world of programming, some motivational examples of individuals who showed incredible will and desire to excel at programming, against all odds..
